Palladium Equity Partners, LLC is the firm's legal name. [firm-profiler-001]

Palladium Equity Partners is a middle-market private equity firm founded in 1997. [firm-profiler-002] The firm is headquartered at 1270 Avenue of the Americas, New York, NY 10020 (31st floor). [firm-profiler-003] Since its founding, Palladium has acquired more than 230–240 businesses across 41 platform investments and completed 200+ add-on acquisitions. [firm-profiler-004] The average tenure of Palladium's senior team is approximately 20 years. [firm-profiler-005]

### Assets Under Management

Palladium's assets under management are approximately $3.6 billion as of December 31, 2025 (Confidence: High). [firm-profiler-006] If adjusted for the funds' use of credit lines, AUM would be reduced by approximately $0.1 billion. [firm-profiler-007] *(Note: a March 2026 press release separately describes AUM more loosely as "over $3 billion" — directionally consistent with, but less precise than, the $3.6B figure above; not independently footnoted as it adds no new information.)*

### Fund Strategy

Palladium's Flagship strategy makes control-oriented equity investments of $50–150M targeting companies with $10–75M in EBITDA. [firm-profiler-008] Fund V, a 2018-vintage fund, raised $1.6 billion; the current fund is Fund VI. [firm-profiler-009] Palladium Heritage is a joint venture focused on the lower middle market, targeting B2B and Industrial companies with $3–12M EBITDA and $10–50M of invested capital. [firm-profiler-010] Palladium's portfolio companies average 4+ add-on acquisitions each, reflecting a buy-and-build growth strategy. [firm-profiler-011]

### Investment Thesis

Palladium has invested approximately $1.5 billion in the U.S. Hispanic market since 2000, and states that 60% of its portfolio companies are U.S. Hispanic businesses. [firm-profiler-017] The firm's strategy centers on providing first institutional capital to founder- and family-owned businesses, often positioning itself as the preferred partner of choice. [firm-profiler-018] Palladium integrates a formal stewardship/ESG framework — including a governance playbook, cybersecurity program, GHG accounting, and 100-day plans — across the investment lifecycle from diligence through exit. [firm-profiler-019] The firm is B Corp certified and a founding signatory of the UN Principles for Responsible Investment (UNPRI). [firm-profiler-020]

### Strategy Synthesis

*(Confidence: Medium — inference layered on multiple underlying claims; Source type: public inference)*

Two observations emerge from comparing Palladium's stated strategy against the observed composition of its 18-company active portfolio, neither of which the firm's own materials state directly:

**1. The claimed U.S. Hispanic-market concentration is not visible in the majority of portfolio company descriptions.** Palladium states that roughly 60% of its portfolio companies operate in or serve the U.S. Hispanic community [firm-profiler-017]. Yet across the 18 active portfolio companies profiled in this dossier, only Del Real Foods [del-real-foods-001] and Quirch Foods [quirch-foods-001] are explicitly described as Hispanic-cuisine or Hispanic-market-focused businesses in their own company materials, with Source Logistics [source-logistics-001] a partial third case (its 3PL client base is described as Hispanic food and beverage brands, not the company itself). That is roughly 3 of 18 companies (~17%) with a source-confirmed Hispanic-market orientation — well short of the firm's stated 60% portfolio-wide figure. This gap could reflect that Hispanic ownership, founder background, or customer concentration for other portfolio companies (e.g., Daniel's, TransForce, Fora Financial) simply isn't disclosed in the one-pager-style materials reviewed here, rather than the 60% figure being inaccurate — but the disconnect between the firm's headline statistic and what is independently observable in the current portfolio's own descriptions is itself a notable, unstated fact.

**2. Disclosed buy-and-build activity is concentrated in two companies, not spread evenly across the portfolio.** Palladium states that its portfolio companies average 4+ add-on acquisitions each [firm-profiler-011]. But of the 18 active companies in this portfolio, only two — Superior Environmental Solutions (6 add-ons since August 2023) [superior-environmental-solutions-009, superior-environmental-solutions-010, superior-environmental-solutions-011, superior-environmental-solutions-012] and Envoy Global (5 add-on acquisitions/expansions since 2021) [envoy-global-007, envoy-global-008, envoy-global-009, envoy-global-010, envoy-global-011] — have any add-on acquisitions documented in the available source set. The other 16 companies (Accupac, Bodewell Group, Collision Auto Parts, Daniel's, Del Real Foods, DME Express, Fora Financial, Health Connect America, Kymera International [only one add-on, Fiven ASA, is on record], Paragon, Quirch Foods, Sky Zone, Skinny Mixes, Spice World, and TransForce) show zero documented add-on transactions in these materials. Combining the firm's stated 4+ average with this observed concentration suggests either that add-on activity for most portfolio companies goes unannounced relative to SES's and Envoy's heavily press-released acquisition programs, or that the buy-and-build thesis is being executed unevenly across the current portfolio — a pattern the firm's own materials do not surface.

## Section 5: Confidence & Source Methodology Summary

This dossier applies three confidence tiers throughout: **High** (a claim is a direct, zero-hop restatement of a single named primary source), **Medium** (the claim requires one traceable inferential hop — e.g., applying a firm-wide theme to a specific company, or inferring portfolio membership from a case-study placement rather than a verbatim statement), and **Low** (the claim is a wide, explicitly directional numeric approximation built from a scale- or business-model-mismatched public comparable, or an order-of-magnitude inference with no direct figure). Two claims (Fora Financial's revenue and EBITDA bands) carry no assigned confidence level at all — they are flagged in the underlying confidence-scoring pass as having an untraceable derivation chain, and are called out explicitly in Section 4 rather than assigned a number that would overstate their reliability.

Three source types appear: **public document** (a named, directly-read primary source — a company one-pager, press release, or the firm's own stewardship report), **public inference** (a conclusion reached by combining or extending named public sources, including all comp-based financial approximations), and two entries with no source type recorded, both explicitly flagged by the source-typer as lacking a clean citation (Daniel's parent-firm claim and TransForce's ownership claim, both in Section 3).

All financial approximations in Section 4 are the product of a private-data-approximation process that prioritizes named, fully-read comparable-company data (SEC filings and company-issued earnings releases) over unverifiable snippets; where no scale-matched comp exists — true for essentially every company in this portfolio, since none are public filers — the resulting band is deliberately widened and labeled **directional** rather than presented with false precision. The one exception where a band was withheld entirely rather than widened is Bodewell Group's revenue and EBITDA (see Section 4), where no verifiable anchor of any kind — not even an approximate one — could be located for the company's own scale.
