# Palladium Equity Partners: Dossier

## Firm Overview

Palladium Equity Partners, LLC is a middle-market private equity firm founded in 1997 and headquartered at 1270 Avenue of the Americas, New York, with additional offices in Stamford, Connecticut and Traverse City, Michigan (pub doc, high; appears on every Palladium press release and the firm website). The firm is chaired and led by founder Marcos A. Rodriguez, and 2026 marks its 29th year in business, which is arithmetically consistent with a 1997 founding (pub doc, high; Palladium Equity Partners.pdf, 2025 Annual Stewardship Report).

Assets under management stand at over $3.6 billion, a figure corroborated by both the Flagship Fact Sheet (March 2026) and the Palladium Heritage One-Pager (pub doc, high). The Flagship Fact Sheet attaches two different "as of" dates to this same $3.6 billion figure in two separate footnotes both numbered "1" on the same page (September 30, 2025 in one instance, December 31, 2025 in the other), which looks like a drafting error rather than a substantive dispute over the number itself; the Palladium Heritage One-Pager independently confirms the figure as of December 31, 2025 (pub doc, low confidence on the exact "as of" date, high confidence on the $3.6 billion headline number itself). Several 2026-dated press releases (the DME Express acquisition release and the Quirch leadership transition release, both dated March 2026) instead describe the firm as having "over $3 billion" under management, which reads as stale boilerplate language carried over from an earlier release rather than a real restatement, since interim press releases from mid-2025 already cited $3.5 billion (pub inf, medium).

Palladium has completed 41 platform investments and more than 200 add-on acquisitions since inception, with roughly 4 or more add-ons per portfolio company on average (pub doc, high; consistent across the Flagship Fact Sheet, Palladium Heritage One-Pager, and firm website). Total acquisition count is reported inconsistently across documents: the 2025 Annual Stewardship Report (April 2026) states "more than 230 businesses," while the DME Express and Quirch Foods press releases (both March 2026) state "more than 240 companies." Both are compatible with an actual total in the low 240s given 41 platforms plus 200+ add-ons, so this reads as imprecise rounding rather than a hard contradiction (pub inf, medium).

The firm has invested approximately $1.5 billion in the U.S. Hispanic market since 2000, representing roughly 60% of its portfolio companies by count (pub doc, high; consistent between the 2025 Annual Stewardship Report and the firm website). Fund V, raised in 2018, has $1.6 billion in committed capital (pub doc, high; Flagship Fact Sheet, Palladium Heritage One-Pager). Fund VI is also active, with a Luxembourg vehicle ("Fund VI Lux") that formalized SFDR Article 8 status in 2024, and DME Express was announced in March 2026 as Fund VI's second platform investment (pub doc, high). No fund-level return statistics (IRR, MOIC, DPI, or similar) appear anywhere in the source set; we could not substantiate a portfolio-wide return-on-investment figure and have left it out rather than estimate one (see Notes for human review).

Palladium is a signatory and founding member of the UN Principles for Responsible Investment, a founding member of the ESG Data Convergence Initiative, a B Corp (certified 2022, recertified 2025 with a 44% score improvement), and a member of the ILPA Diversity in Action initiative and the Global Impact Investing Network (pub doc, high; 2025 Annual Stewardship Report).

## Investment Criteria

Palladium's Flagship strategy targets control-oriented transactions (LBO/MBO structures using common equity, preferred equity, structured capital, or a combination, including majority or minority positions and restructurings) in companies with $10 million to $75 million of EBITDA (no minimum for add-on acquisitions), using $50 million to $150 million of equity capital per platform (pub doc, high; consistent across the Flagship Fact Sheet and the firm's website strategy page). Geographic focus is the United States. Capital is deployed for founder transitions, management buyouts, growth capital, corporate divestitures, recapitalizations, restructurings, rescue financings, and add-on acquisitions (pub doc, high).

Key industries are consumer, healthcare, industrials, and services, with detailed sub-vertical themes published in the Flagship Fact Sheet, including food safety/testing/certification, clean-label supply chain, home health/hospice, physician practice management, specialty distribution, HVAC, route-based services, and IT/BPO services, among many others (pub doc, high). On the business-characteristics side, Palladium's public materials emphasize founder- and family-owned businesses as a preferred deal source, noting it is "often the first institutional capital" such businesses receive, and emphasizes accretive, buy-and-build M&A as a repeatable value lever across its portfolio (pub doc, high; firm website, Flagship strategy page). We did not find a standalone cyclicity analysis of target end-markets in the source set; the closest proxy is the sector list itself, which skews toward recurring-revenue, non-discretionary service categories (healthcare services, environmental/industrial services, specialty distribution), suggesting a preference for defensive, less cyclical business models (pub inf, medium).

Separately, Palladium Heritage, a joint venture with Palladium focused on the lower middle market, targets smaller companies with $3 million to $12 million of EBITDA (no minimum for add-ons), deploying $10 million to $50 million of invested capital for majority control or significant minority positions, concentrated in business-to-business and industrial sectors across North America (pub doc, high; Palladium Heritage One-Pager). Transaction types mirror Flagship's (entrepreneur-backed growth, management buyouts, corporate divestitures) but at smaller check sizes.

## Investment Approach and Specialty

Palladium describes itself as a thematic investor that "capitalizes on emerging economic trends," organizing its Flagship investment teams by sector to identify niche trends within each vertical rather than screening opportunistically (pub doc, high; firm website, Flagship strategy page). Three cross-cutting themes recur across its materials: evolving/reshoring supply chains, health and wellness, and the U.S. Hispanic market, the last of which the firm treats as a differentiated sourcing edge built on two decades of relationships and reputation within Hispanic-founder and Hispanic-consumer-facing businesses (pub doc, high).

Post-acquisition value creation runs on a structured "assess, measure, improve" cycle applied across the full deal lifecycle, from origination and diligence through the hold period and exit (pub doc, high; 2025 Annual Stewardship Report). New platforms go through a 100-day plan that sets initial stewardship and operating priorities, including a baseline GHG emissions assessment and an assigned internal stewardship owner, sometimes formalized through a dedicated board committee (pub doc, high). A firm-wide Corporate Governance Playbook, rolled out portfolio-wide in 2025, standardizes policies on employee conduct, labor and human rights, and anti-corruption, which Palladium frames explicitly as preparing "entrepreneurial businesses" to become "institutional-grade platforms capable of attracting both strategic and institutional buyers at exit" (pub doc, high). This is a fairly direct statement that governance standardization is itself an exit-readiness lever, not just a compliance exercise (pub inf, medium).

Palladium also runs a portfolio-wide cybersecurity program supported by third-party providers and an AI Task Force organized around four workstreams (Integrate, Automate, Augment, Codify), with generative AI tool use gated by Chief Compliance Officer approval following third-party diligence via Drawbridge (pub doc, high; 2025 Annual Stewardship Report). One concrete example the firm cites is applying its own stewardship-assessment framework via an AI agent during diligence, which it says cut certain diligence workstreams from about a week to under an hour (pub doc, medium, single source, self-reported by Palladium without independent verification).

Approach to incumbent management is founder-friendly rather than replacement-driven in framing: the firm repeatedly emphasizes partnering with existing founders and management teams (DME Express, SES, Quirch, Hydroblasters, J&S, ChemWorx press releases all quote continuing founder-operators), and Quirch's 2026 CEO transition was explicitly structured as a multi-year internal succession (COO promoted to President, then CEO) rather than an external replacement, with the outgoing CEO retained on the board (pub doc, high).

For growth-through-acquisition specifically, Superior Environmental Solutions (SES) is Palladium's clearest illustration: since Palladium's investment in August 2023, SES has completed at least four add-on acquisitions (Captain Clean, Hydroblasters, J&S Industrial Services, ChemWorx) in roughly two and a half years, each expanding either geographic footprint or technical capability (pub doc, high; four separate SES press releases, 2025 to 2026).

## Current Portfolio Companies

Fund-level attribution is only explicitly documented for one active company in our source set (DME Express, tied to Fund VI). The Flagship Fact Sheet's portfolio table draws on Funds II, III, IV, V, and VI collectively without breaking out which fund holds which currently active company, and no other source assigns individual portfolio companies to specific fund vintages. We have organized the list below by strategy vehicle and sector instead of by fund, and flag the fund-attribution gap under Notes for human review.

### Flagship Strategy

**Healthcare**

Health Connect America (Franklin, TN) is a mission-driven provider of in-home, community-based, and school-based mental and behavioral health services, predominantly for adolescents and young adults, covering conduct disorder treatment, family counseling, autism services, and medication management across Tennessee, Virginia, Georgia, Alabama, North Carolina, Florida, and Mississippi, with more than 1,700 employees (pub doc, medium, single source). Under Palladium's stewardship program, the company overhauled its talent-acquisition process (AI-enabled candidate screening, structured behavioral interviews, defined hiring KPIs) and expanded retention programming; its 2025 voluntary turnover rate fell 8% year over year and profit per employee rose nearly 3x between Q4 2024 and Q4 2025 (pub doc, medium, single source, self-reported). Its EcoVadis stewardship scores improved substantially against a 2022 baseline: overall +82%, Environment +113%, Sustainable Procurement +150%, Labor & Human Rights +60%, Ethics +45% (pub doc, medium; Palladium discloses this case was "selected based on non-performance-based criteria" rather than as a representative sample, which should temper how much weight is put on it).

DME Express (The Woodlands, TX), founded in 2006, is a provider of durable medical equipment and logistics services to hospice providers, delivering beds, respiratory equipment, mobility products, and support surfaces under recurring per-patient-day contracts across 9 states through a network of more than 70 warehouse locations (pub doc, high; corroborated by both the DME Express acquisition press release and the firm's own portfolio page). Palladium announced an agreement in March 2026 to acquire a majority equity interest from WayPoint Capital Partners, which retains a minority stake; founder and CEO Mark Borneleit continues to lead the business. This is Palladium's second hospice-industry investment and the second platform investment of Fund VI. The press release notes closing was "subject to the satisfaction of certain conditions," and we found no later source confirming the deal has closed (pub doc, medium, on transaction status specifically).

**Consumer**

Sky Zone (Provo, UT) operates experiential entertainment parks under the Sky Zone, Defy, and Rockin' Jump brands, with approximately 300 parks in the U.S. and internationally, featuring trampolines, ninja courses, foam pits, and dodgeball (pub doc, medium, single source).

Daniel's Jewelers (Culver City, CA) is a tech-enabled jewelry retailer operating more than 100 retail locations across the continental U.S. plus a growing e-commerce platform; company branding indicates it has operated since 1948 (pub doc, medium, single source).

**Food**

Quirch Foods (Miami, FL), founded in 1967, is a distributor, importer, and exporter of protein and Hispanic food products serving independent and ethnic grocers, foodservice operators, and export markets across the U.S., Puerto Rico, and Latin America, operating 24 distribution facilities (2.4 million square feet of refrigerated warehouse space) and a fleet of more than 400 trucks (pub doc, high; corroborated across three separate press releases). Palladium first invested in 2018, when Quirch's annual sales were approximately $1 billion; by November 2025, annual sales exceeded $4 billion, implying roughly 4x sales growth over the seven-year hold to date (pub doc on the two data points, high; the 4x growth-multiple framing is pub inf, medium). In November 2025, Quirch completed a $1.1 billion strategic debt refinancing led by Ares U.S. Direct Lending funds and Regions Bank, expanding its asset-based lending facility and adding a delayed-draw term loan earmarked for brand, talent, technology investment, and further acquisitions (pub doc, high). In March 2026, the company executed a planned leadership transition: CEO Frank Grande moved to the board (chairing the M&A Committee) and President/COO Joe Ward, who joined as COO in late 2024 and was named President in 2025, became President and CEO (pub doc, high).

Skinny Mixes (Jordan's Skinny Mixes, Clearwater, FL) manufactures low-calorie cocktail mixes, zero-sugar coffee and fruit syrups, and related specialty beverage products, sold in more than 15,000 retail locations across the U.S., Canada, Australia, and Europe (pub doc, medium, single source).

Spice World (Orlando, FL), founded around 1949 ("more than 70 years ago" as of the current source date), is a fresh flavorings supplier focused on the U.S. retail produce aisle, built around garlic, ginger, and flavor-blend formats (pub doc, high, internally consistent between the "est. 1949" branding and the "70+ years" text). In 2025, the company began selling previously discarded, off-spec garlic (more than 4,500 tons) to a cattle-feed company instead of paying a third party to dispose of it, converting a cost center into a new revenue stream (pub doc, medium, single source).

Source Logistics (Houston, TX), acquired by Palladium in 2023, provides value-added warehousing, distribution, and fulfillment (3PL) services to the consumer goods sector, with a focus on Hispanic food and beverage brands, operating more than 5.0 million square feet of facility space across Texas, California, Illinois, New Jersey, and Oregon (pub doc, high; corroborated across the firm's portfolio page and the 2025 Annual Stewardship Report). Since acquisition, the company has expanded its Midwest operations, doubled its warehouse space, and can now serve 73% of the U.S. population within one day's transit; EBITDA has more than doubled and revenue grew just under 90% year over year in 2025 (pub doc, medium; Palladium discloses this case was "selected based on non-performance-based criteria," which we read as a caveat against treating it as typical portfolio performance). In 2026, the company rolled out a company-wide safety program, including bilingual role-based training and expanded OSHA certification (pub doc, medium, single source).

**Services**

Superior Environmental Solutions, or SES (headquartered in Cincinnati, OH per its own portfolio page, though acquisition press releases are dated from Cedar Rapids, IA and Wisconsin Rapids, WI), founded in 1999, provides mission-critical, non-discretionary environmental and industrial services, including high-pressure water jetting, vacuum truck services, waste management, product destruction, and tank cleaning to agriculture, food processing, chemicals, metals, utilities, automotive, and refining customers (pub doc, high; consistent founding year across five separate documents). Palladium has owned SES since August 2023 and has supported an active buy-and-build program: Captain Clean (Cedar Rapids, IA-area; founded 1969), acquired May 2025, SES's 9th acquisition overall and 3rd under Palladium; Hydroblasters Inc. (Wisconsin Rapids, WI; founded 1995), acquired July 2025, SES's 10th overall and 4th under Palladium; J&S Industrial Services (Greater St. Louis, MO; founded 2020), acquired November 2025, SES's 11th overall and 5th under Palladium; and ChemWorx (Decatur, AL; founded 2020), acquired February 2026, SES's 12th acquisition overall (pub doc, high; each drawn from its own dedicated press release). Scale figures are reported inconsistently across sources: SES's own portfolio page describes "over 500 employees," "over 500 customers," and "more than 25 locations," while press releases from May and July 2025 describe "nearly 1,000 employees" and 64 to 65 facilities across 14 states. We read the portfolio-page figures as an older, unrefreshed snapshot predating the 2025 to 2026 acquisition run, rather than a genuine dispute about SES's current size (pub inf, low confidence on the smaller figures specifically; the ~1,000-employee, 64 to 65-location figures should be treated as current).

Envoy Global (Chicago, IL) provides immigration software and workforce management for global enterprises, combining proprietary technology with legal representation across more than 180 countries; CEO is Richard Burke (pub doc, medium, single source on the core description). The company has pursued an active international buy-and-build strategy of its own since 2022, acquiring UAE-based Sesam Immigration (August 2023), UK-based Smith Stone Walters (October 2024), South Africa/Kenya/Namibia-based IBN Immigration Solutions (early 2025), and Frankfurt-based Offer & Mastmann (July 2025, founded 2005), while also expanding organically into Saudi Arabia, France, Spain, and the Netherlands (pub doc, high).

Fora Financial (New York, NY), founded in June 2008, is a tech-enabled lending platform offering flexible working-capital solutions to small and medium-sized businesses; it has more than 200 employees and has provided nearly $2 billion to borrowers across all 50 states, operating out of New York, Miami, and Santa Ana, CA (pub doc, medium, single source).

TransForce (Alexandria, VA) is a tech-enabled workforce solutions firm for the transportation and logistics industry, organized around three service lines (Employment Solutions, Digital Solutions, Education & Training) serving 3PLs, private fleets, insurers, local governments, and veterans (pub doc, medium, single source).

**Industrials**

Paragon (Houston, TX) is an oilfield services company operating in the Northeast and Permian regions, providing drilling fluids, solids control, and environmental services to oil and gas and industrial customers (pub doc, medium, single source).

Kymera International (Research Triangle Park, NC) develops and manufactures advanced specialty materials and high-performance surface coatings for aerospace and defense, automotive, building and construction, chemical, industrial, electronics/semiconductors, medical, and energy end markets (pub doc, medium, single source). Its safety metrics (Total Recordable Incident Rate) improved from 2023 through 2025, trending toward its BLS industry benchmark for secondary smelting, refining, and alloying of nonferrous metal (pub doc, medium, single source).

Accupac (Mainland, PA), founded in 1974, is a brand-development partner providing formulation, manufacturing, and packaging for the personal care and beauty industry, including FDA-regulated products such as skin creams, toothpaste, and sunscreens; the company employs almost 1,000 people across two Mid-Atlantic manufacturing facilities (pub doc, medium, single source). Its R&D arm, Cobalt Labs, develops sustainable product formulas for customers; 85% of Accupac's new product formulas in 2025 met its sustainability criteria, against a 50% internal target (pub doc, medium, single source).

## Historic Exited Investments

The Flagship Fact Sheet's portfolio table (as of December 31, 2025) lists a substantial number of exited names across every sector, including Jordan Health Services and HealthSun Health Plans (healthcare), La Vaquita, Good West, Teasdale, Del Real, Second Nature Brands, and Taco Bueno (food), everdriven, Regional Management, Pronto Insurance, Capital Contractors, DolEx Financial Services, Cannella Media, ANC, and Raben Tire (services), and Trachte, Prince, and American Gilsonite (industrials). Beyond their names appearing in this logo grid, we have no descriptive source material for any of them, so we can only write a substantive synopsis for one.

Del Real Foods (Mira Loma, CA) is a manufacturer of traditionally inspired, branded and private-label, heat-and-serve Hispanic cuisine, including value-added meats, prepared specialty items, fresh salsas, and side dishes, sold through club, retail, foodservice, and wholesale distributor channels; the company is the largest producer of fresh tamales in the country, selling more than 80 million tamales annually (pub doc, medium, single source). We flag a status conflict here: the Flagship Fact Sheet classifies Del Real Foods as an exited investment as of December 31, 2025, but the company's own page remains live on Palladium's website in the same present-tense, active-investment format used for currently held companies, with no exit language. The most likely explanation is that Palladium keeps case-study pages live for marketing purposes after an exit, but we have not confirmed that, so the "exited" classification here should be treated as low confidence pending adjudication.
